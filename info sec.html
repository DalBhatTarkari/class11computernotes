Computer Ethics

Set of moral principles that we need to follow while being in digital space.
Examples:

  • Don't use computer to harm others.
  • Don't use computer to spread false information.
  • Don't use computer to perform harassment, bullying etc.

Cyber crimes

Cybercrimes are illegal activities that involve computers, networks, or digital systems to steal, harm, or exploit individuals, organizations, or governments. This involves deception, unauthorized access, or intellectual dishonesty.

Examples of Cybercrimes

Hacking

Hacking is the act of gaining unauthorized access to computer systems, networks, or information. It can be ethical (for security testing) or malicious.

Types of hackers:

  • White hat hacker: Hacker who legally breaks into systems, and finds and fixes system vulnerabilities.
  • Black hat hacker: Malicious hacker who illegally exploits system weaknesses for personal gain, fraud, data theft, etc.
  • Grey hat hacker: Someone who finds and exploits security vulnerabilities without permission but does not do so for malicious purposes, informing organizations about security issues to pressure fixes.
  • Script Kiddie: Inexperienced hacker using pre-made tools without deep knowledge.
  • Hacktivist: Hacker who uses cyberattacks for political or social activism and promotes political, social, or ideological causes.

Plagiarism

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work, ideas, or content without proper credit, and presenting it as your own.

Types of plagiarism:

  • Direct Plagiarism: Copying word-for-word without citation.
  • Self-Plagiarism: Reusing one's own previous work without disclosure.
  • Paraphrasing Plagiarism: Rewriting someoneâ€™s work with some changes without credit.
  • Mosaic Plagiarism: Mixing phrases from different sources without citation.
  • Accidental Plagiarism: Failing to cite sources due to ignorance or oversight.

Phishing

Phishing scams are attempts by scammers to trick people into giving sensitive information like bank account numbers, passwords, credit card numbers, etc.

Cyber extortion

A crime involving an attack or threat of an attack coupled with a demand for money to stop the attack.

Common examples:

  • Ransomware Attacks: Attacker gains access to a user's or organization's system, encrypts the data, and demands payment (usually in the form of cryptocurrency) to decrypt the data.
  • DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) Extortion: Attacker threatens to overload a website or system unless paid.
  • Data Breach Blackmail: Stealing sensitive data and demanding ransom to prevent exposure.
  • Email Sextortion: Scammers claim to have compromising images and demand payment to not leak the images.
